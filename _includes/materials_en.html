PLA Silk

What is PLA Silk? ✨

PLA Silk is a special variant of PLA that achieves an extreme, silky shine through additives. It is excellent for decorative objects as it makes layers almost invisible, but it is mechanically slightly less resilient (lower layer adhesion) than standard PLA.

PLA & Environment 🌍

Sustainability is important to me ♻️. Almost all of my models are printed using PLA (Polylactic Acid).

  • Renewable Resources 🌱: PLA is derived from plant-based resources like corn starch or sugar cane.
  • Biodegradable ♻️: Under industrial composting conditions, PLA is biodegradable.
  • Eco-conscious 🍃: Compared to traditional petroleum-based plastics, the production of PLA has a significantly lower carbon footprint.
  • Not UV-resistant ☀️: Therefore, it should not be used for permanent, direct sunlight exposure. (Behind a window is no problem, as the glass filters UV rays)

PETG

What is PETG? 🧪

PETG (Polyethylene Terephthalate Glycol) is a versatile plastic that combines the best properties of PLA and ABS.

  • Robust & Flexible: It is significantly more impact-resistant and tougher than PLA, meaning it breaks less easily. It is used when mechanical stress is common (e.g., a phone holder in a car).
  • Heat Resistant: PETG can withstand temperatures up to approx. 75-80°C, making it suitable for use in cars (as long as you don't plan to park the car in the Sahara 😉).
  • Recyclability: PETG is fully recyclable and belongs to the polyester family.
  • Not UV-resistant ☀️: Therefore, it should not be used for permanent, direct sunlight exposure. (Behind a window is no problem, as the glass filters UV rays)
  • Chemical Resistance: It is resistant to many chemicals and weathering effects.
